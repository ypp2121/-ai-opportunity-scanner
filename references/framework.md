# Analytical Framework: AI Cost Inversion

## The Core Equation

**Viability Threshold = (Revenue from task) / (Fully-loaded cost to complete task)**

Before AI, many tasks sit below 1.0 (not viable). AI changes this by driving the denominator toward zero for the *marginal* task.

Important distinction:
- **Fixed costs** may remain (setup, training, infrastructure)
- **Marginal costs** per task collapse
- Therefore, the winning formula is usually **high volume of small tasks** or **high frequency of repeated decisions**

## Six Lenses for Analysis

Apply these lenses when scanning an industry. A strong opportunity usually scores well on 2+ lenses.

### Lens 1: Information Asymmetry
Where do players make bad decisions because gathering/processing information is too expensive?
- Examples: policy monitoring, supplier benchmarking, price comparison, risk scanning
- AI unlocks: continuous monitoring + summarization + translation

### Lens 2: Customization Burden
Where does every customer/project require a bespoke document, design, or calculation?
- Examples: proposals, engineering drawings, legal contracts, insurance quotes
- AI unlocks: template-to-customized-document generation at near-zero marginal cost

### Lens 3: Expert Bottleneck
Where is a scarce expert (engineer, lawyer, doctor, underwriter) the gatekeeper?
- Examples: technical feasibility studies, contract review, medical triage, due diligence
- AI unlocks: draft generation and first-pass screening, reserving experts for exceptions

### Lens 4: Data Deluge
Where is valuable data generated but never analyzed because volume exceeds human capacity?
- Examples: IoT sensor streams, maintenance logs, customer service transcripts, financial transactions
- AI unlocks: pattern recognition, anomaly detection, predictive scoring

### Lens 5: Coordination Friction
Where do multiple parties need to align on complex, interdependent documents or plans?
- Examples: construction drawings, supply chain contracts, clinical trials, cross-border trade
- AI unlocks: automatic conflict detection, version reconciliation, multi-party summarization

### Lens 6: Geographic / Linguistic Fragmentation
Where are markets ignored because the cost of localization exceeds expected returns?
- Examples: emerging market expansion, multilingual support, regional regulatory compliance
- AI unlocks: real-time translation, local policy parsing, culturally adapted content

## Value Chain Scanning Method

For any industry, walk through these stages and ask: *What was too expensive to do per-unit?*

| Stage | Questions |
|-------|-----------|
| **R&D / Design** | What custom calculations or simulations were skipped for small variants? |
| **Sourcing / Procurement** | What supplier evaluation or contract review was done only for big orders? |
| **Manufacturing / Operations** | What quality checks or process optimizations were too labor-intensive? |
| **Sales / Marketing** | What lead qualification or proposal customization was reserved for whales? |
| **Delivery / Implementation** | What site surveys, designs, or commissioning steps were simplified for small projects? |
| **Operations / Maintenance** | What predictive maintenance or performance optimization was ignored? |
| **Finance / Compliance** | what audits, certifications, or risk assessments were skipped for small assets? |
| **End-of-Life** | What resale, recycling, or warranty claims processing was too expensive? |

---

## The "Chain Store" Test

A quick heuristic for spotting opportunity:

> *If an industry has many small, geographically distributed, operationally similar units (chain stores, branch offices, small factories, cell towers, rental properties), AI-enabled standardization + remote analysis almost always unlocks value.*

These are classic "engineer won't fly there" scenarios.
