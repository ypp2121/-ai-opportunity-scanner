# 12 AI Cost-Inversion Archetypes

Use these as a checklist when scanning any industry.

---

## Pattern 1: The Long-Tail Project
**Before**: Expert time per project > project margin. Only whales were served.  
**After**: AI automates feasibility, design, or proposal generation. Small projects become profitable at scale.  
**Examples**: small commercial solar, chain-store energy retrofits, boutique legal matters, small business loans.

## Pattern 2: The Continuous Radar
**Before**: Monitoring 50+ data sources (policies, competitors, prices) required a team.  
**After**: AI agents scrape, translate, and summarize 24/7. One person manages the signal.  
**Examples**: regulatory intelligence, commodity price tracking, patent monitoring, brand reputation.

## Pattern 3: The Document Factory
**Before**: Every transaction required a bespoke report, contract, or certificate.  
**After**: AI generates first drafts from structured data, humans review exceptions.  
**Examples**: insurance policies, compliance reports, technical certifications, RFP responses.

## Pattern 4: The Expert Triage
**Before**: Scarce experts spent 80% of time on tasks that didn't need their full judgment.  
**After**: AI handles first-pass screening, routing, and routine answers. Experts handle edge cases.  
**Examples**: medical diagnosis triage, legal contract review, engineering design review, underwriting.

## Pattern 5: The Dormant Data Mine
**Before**: Valuable data was collected but never analyzed due to volume or noise.  
**After**: AI extracts patterns, anomalies, and predictions at machine scale.  
**Examples**: equipment sensor data, customer service logs, satellite imagery, financial transactions.

## Pattern 6: The Cross-Border Unlock
**Before**: Entering a new market required translators, local researchers, and regulatory consultants.  
**After**: AI localizes documents, parses foreign regulations, and adapts messaging in hours.  
**Examples**: product manuals, export compliance, multilingual sales, emerging market expansion.

## Pattern 7: The Knowledge Archaeologist
**Before**: Organizational expertise was trapped in emails, reports, and retired employees' heads.  
**After**: AI indexes unstructured archives and surfaces relevant precedent on demand.  
**Examples**: engineering failure analysis, legal precedent search, sales playbook generation, maintenance history.

## Pattern 8: The Conflict Detective
**Before**: Aligning multi-party documents or plans required hours of manual cross-referencing.  
**After**: AI flags inconsistencies, version conflicts, and compliance gaps automatically.  
**Examples**: construction drawings, supply chain contracts, clinical trial protocols, M&A due diligence.

## Pattern 9: The Personalized Pitch
**Before**: Customizing a proposal or recommendation for a small customer was uneconomical.  
**After**: AI tailors pitches, curricula, or treatment plans using customer-specific data.  
**Examples**: B2B proposals, educational tutoring, personalized medicine, retail financial advice.

## Pattern 10: The Idle Asset Matchmaker
**Before**: Matching underutilized assets to demand was too information-intensive.  
**After**: AI predicts availability, prices dynamically, and automates matching.  
**Examples**: freight logistics, warehouse space, computing power, rental equipment, grid-scale batteries.

## Pattern 11: The Compliance Tax Eliminator
**Before**: Regulatory filings, certifications, and audits consumed disproportionate overhead for small players.  
**After**: AI auto-populates forms, tracks requirement changes, and prepares audit evidence.  
**Examples**: carbon credit certification, food safety audits, export licenses, tax compliance.

## Pattern 12: The Simulation Democratizer
**Before**: Physics-based or financial modeling required expensive software and specialized operators.  
**After**: AI-assisted modeling allows non-experts to run sophisticated what-if scenarios.  
**Examples**: energy system modeling, supply chain stress tests, drug interaction modeling, construction sequencing.
